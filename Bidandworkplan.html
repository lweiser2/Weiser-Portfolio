Advanced GIS Final Project Proposal

Overview:

My plan is to build a drug overdose prediction model for the Allegheny Department of Public Health. In recent years, the opioid epidemic has reached new heights along with the continued issue of illegal heroin abuse. Overdoses often occur in clusters, as drug users commonly share substances and other materials and may use drugs in groups. The increase in overdoses over the past several years is likely due to the increased spread of fentanyl-laced drugs which require lower doses to cause an overdose. Using past data which includes the zipcodes in which overdoses occurred, I am going to create a predictive model to determine where future overdose clusters may pop up. This could help the Department of Public Health and its collaborators be more proactive about preventing overdoses, as well as be able to react and provide emergency services faster. If the model is appropriately accurate (r^2 greater than .5), it could be helpful in ascertaining where these services are most needed and should be deployed.

The deliverables for this project will include GIS analysis with a corresponding dashboard to explain and visualize the results to those who are not familiar with predictive models and/or GIS. The dashboard will display the map of predicted overdoses along with a clear description of how accurate it is and how it can be used.
